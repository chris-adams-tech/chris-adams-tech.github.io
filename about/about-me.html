About Me

So, what's on this website? 🤔

This website is a repository of my notes some from school back when earning my associates in Cyber Defense, cyber projects, deep dives, and course from certifications. As you may notice, some of the pages are incomplete and I often will start a page, then revisit. This is just a part of my process, as I am continuously integrating, improving, and reindexing the data held within the site.

Who am I?

Now a bit about myself, I'm relatively new to the cyber security industry, I have worked just over a year as a Triage Security Engineer at a Managed Security Services Provider in a Security Operations Center. Since getting into the industry my passion has grown tremendously. I didn't realize that my passion would just continue to grow! It's like that never ending puzzle that continuously keeps my brain challenged, I never have to be bored or think...hmmm.....what should I learn? It seems that Active Directory related tasks continue to come to me...in 2023 my college team and I placed 2nd in a cyber competition defending against a red team and you guessed it, I was the Active Directory guy! Once I got into triage, I noticed how many incidents are related to Initial Access, which led me to studying more concepts on Active Directory, as that is one of the most utilized services used today.

I also like challenging climbs, well anything challenging excites me, probably why I'm in tech.

This was me in Italy not too long ago, casually climbing mountain peaks in Tevas ⛰️

Anything outdoors excites me, but there is a time for play and a time for work. Maintaining that balance is crucial and pertinent to maintaining a healthy balance.

This year I had the opportunity to collaborate with senior members of the organization I was working with to be on the team to put together a Red & Blue team village. Through this experience I learned so much about virtual bridging and using CLI with KVM, AD server GPO and logging configuration, Sysmon configuration, Wazuh agent and manager deployment configuration and indexer configuration, Linux file structures, Sysmon xml configuration and deployment, deploying Sysmon with GPO, Active Directory pentesting, and detection engineering. Though there was many hours put in and did not get paid, every single mistake turned into a learning lesson and that's a win in my book. I have continued to learn more and expand upon what was learned during that time.

Fast forward to now and I feel as though I've completed a system of holistic learning. I have become a more functional learner, rather than the linear, top to bottom learner that most formal schooling might teach. Though I did earn a formal degree, an A.A.S. degree in Cyber Defense, I am not entirely sold on the whole I'm going to get get a book and study for the multiple choice exam. To me, this makes sense in a very foundational perspective, however, when delving into more complex topics, a read from the book and answer some multiple choice questions, doesn't seem to stimulate my brain in the way that something like that could.

That's sort of what brought on this whole project. This was a way for me to document my learning process and in that would create many pages that could be used for others to learn as well. Maybe there are some out there that will also see this as a tool with a lot of potential and could evolve into a new style of framework of learning, I really don't know, but it has worked for me thus far and am excited to expand and evolve the project.

Lately, I have been diving more into automation, development, engineering, partly in relation to cloud (AWS), and working on HacktheBox: Cyber Defense Security Analyst certification and the AWS: Cloud Practitioner.
