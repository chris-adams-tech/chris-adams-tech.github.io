Cyber Kill Chain

Cyber Kill Chain Overview

The Cyber Kill Chain is a model developed by Lockheed Martin to describe the stages that adversaries follow when executing a cyber attack. The goal of the model is to help defenders identify and disrupt the attack at various stages before it reaches the intended target. Think of it as a series of checkpoints in an attacker's journey—if defenders can catch the attacker at any point, they can prevent or mitigate damage.

The seven stages of the Cyber Kill Chain are:

Reconnaissance:

The attacker gathers information about the target (e.g., researching the network, identifying vulnerable systems, or employees to target).

Weaponization:

The attacker creates a deliverable payload, such as a malware-laden document or a malicious script that can be executed on the target system.

Delivery:

The method by which the malicious payload is sent to the victim. This could be phishing emails, malicious websites, USB drops, or other means.

Exploitation:

After the delivery, the payload exploits a vulnerability to gain a foothold, such as exploiting a software vulnerability to run code or gain higher privileges.

Installation:

Once the exploit is successful, the attacker installs a backdoor or malware on the system to maintain persistent access.

Command and Control (C2):

The attacker establishes communication with the compromised system to remotely control it and issue commands, often via C2 servers.

Actions on Objectives:

Finally, the attacker carries out their goal, whether it’s data exfiltration, ransomware deployment, system disruption, or another malicious intent.
