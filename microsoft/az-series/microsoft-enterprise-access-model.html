Attack Vectors

Each of these planes has control of the data and workloads by virtue of their functions, creating an attractive pathway for attackers to abuse if they can gain control of either plane.

To implement, systems must be accessible to internal users, partners, and customers using their workstations or devices

  • systems must also frequently be available via API to facilitate process automation
    • creates application access pathways

The last step to ensure the organizations security gap is minimized, privileged access pathways may be created

By providing this consistent access, there are additional requirements to ensure your organization efficiently enables productivity, while also mitigating associated risks.

Evolution of the legacy AD tier model

Tier 0 scope expansion
  • control plane and addresses all aspects of access control, including networking, where it is the only/best access control option, such as legacy OT options

Tier 1 splits

  • Management plane* - for enterprise-wide IT management functions
  • Data/Workload plane - for pre-workload management, sometimes performed by IT personnel and sometimes by business units
T1 Split

This split ensures focus for protecting business critical systems and administrative roles that have high intrinsic business value, but limited technical control. Additionally, this split better accommodates developers and DevOps models vs. focusing too heavily on classic infrastructure roles.

Tier 2 splits

  • User access* - includes all B2B, B2C, and public access scenarios
  • App access - to accommodate API access pathways and resulting attack surface

Privileged Access : Strategy

Privileged access

Privileged access should be the top security priority at every organization.
This strategy is built on Zero Trust principles of explicit validation, least privilege, and assumption of breach. Microsoft provides implementation guidance to help you rapidly deploy protections based on this strategy

High Business Impact

  • Targeted data theft* #exfiltration - attackers use privileged access to steal sensitive intellectual property for their own use or to sell/transfer to your competitors or foreign governments
  • Human-operated ransomeware (HumOR) #ransomware - attacks use privileged access to steal and/or encrypt all data and systems in enterprise, often stopping all business operations and demanding money to not disclose data and/or providing keys to unlock
    • https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2020/03/05/human-operated-ransomware-attacks-a-preventable-disaster/

Holistic Practical Strategy

Strategy

You must adopt a strategy that includes multiple technologies to defend against these attacks. Simply implementing a privileged identity management / privileged access management (PIM/PAM) solution is not sufficient. For more information, see, Privileged access Intermediaries.

Cloud services create security

  • has better capabilities*
  • is easier and faster
  • requires less maintenance
  • cloud keeps improving

Two Pathways for accessing systems

  • Privileged Access* - IT administrators or other sensitive accounts access business-critical systems and data or perform administrative tasks on enterprise systems

The full access management systems also includes identity systems and authorized elevation paths

  • Identity Systems* - provide identity directories that host the accounts and administrative groups, synchronization and federation capabilities, and other identity support functions for standard and privileged users
  • Authorized Elevation Paths - provide a means for standard users to interact with privileged workflows, such as managers or peers approving requests for administrative rights to a sensitive system through a just-in-time (JIT) process in PAM/PIM system
Note

For on-premises and infrastructure as a service (IaaS) systems hosted on a customer-managed operating system, the attack surface dramatically increases with management and security agents, service accounts, and potential configuration issues.

For best practice, implement:

  • Zero Trust Access control* - includes rapid modernization plan (RAMP)
  • Asset Protection - to protect against direct asset attacks by applying good security hygiene practices

Strategy Initiatives

  • End-to-end session security* - implement Zero Trust validation for privileged sessions, user sessions, and authorized elevation paths
    • each session validates that each user account and device are trusted at a sufficient level before allowing access
  • Protect & Monitor Identity Systems - including Directories, Identity Management, Admin Accounts, Consent grants, and more
    • each system protected at a level appropriate for potential business impact of accounts hosted on it
  • Mitigate Lateral Traversal - to protect against lateral traversal with local account passwords, service account passwords, or other secrets
    • compromising a single device won't immediately lead to control of many or all other devices in the environment
  • Rapid Threat Response - to limit adversary access and time in the environment
    • stops adversaries from reliably conducting a multi-stage attack in the environment that would result in loss of privileged access
      • Measured by reducing the mean time to remediate (MTTR) of incidents involving privileged access to near zero and reducing MTTR of all incidents to a few minutes so adversaries don't have time to target privileged access.
