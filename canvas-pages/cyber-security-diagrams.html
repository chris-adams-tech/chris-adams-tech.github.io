Challenges in Identity Management

Identity Theft and Fraud

  • when unauthorized individuals gain access to sensitive information

Managing Identities Across Multiple Platforms

  • unique protocols for each platform makes it difficult to maintain consistency in IM

Managing RBAC in Large Organizations

  • With numerous users, roles, and permissions to manage, the risk of errors and inconsistencies is high.

Principle of Least Privilege

  • minimum necessary privileges

Single Sign-On (SSO)

  • users authenticate once to access multiple resources
  • enhances both convenience and security*

Identity and Access Management

Compliance with Privacy Regulations

  • requires a deep understanding of various regulations
  • ability to effectively implement them in the identity management framework

Balancing Security and User Experience

  • strict security measures may protect against threats, but may come at the cost of the user experience.
  • Too many security layers can frustrate users
  • compromising security for a smoother experience can expose the organization to potential threats

Managing Roles and Permissions

  • large number of users and constantly changing roles, managing these permissions can be a daunting task

Zero Trust

  • trust is not assumed based on location or network
  • every access is verified, even in business perimeter

Role Based Access Control (RBAC)

  • based on predefined roles
